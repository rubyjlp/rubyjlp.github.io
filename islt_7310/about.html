Ruby Landau-Pincus

About

Ruby Landau-Pincus is an archivist and artist living and working in New York City. She works as the Reference and Outreach Archivist at the YIVO Institute for Jewish Research. She is a graduate student in library science through the University of Missouri. She graduated with her bachelor’s degree in Yiddish studies from Columbia University in 2022.
In her art practice, Ruby works across a range of disciplines. Her body of work includes plays, poetry, music, comics, and collage. Her work explores themes of lesbianism, neurodivergence, and chronic illness. She often takes inspiration from Jewish and Yiddish culture. Her most recent work, Tunnel Play, was written in collaboration with Charlotte Teresa Guerrero. It premiered at a night of new theater by the Spade Collective at Brick Aux in March 2024. The script is available through New Play Exchange.
Ruby lives in Brighton Beach, Brooklyn. She loves spending time with her very large cat, playing games with friends, and trying new foods.
