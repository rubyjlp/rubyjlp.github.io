Tunnel Play, co-written with Charlotte Teresa Guerrero

Charlotte Teresa Guerrero as Tunnel Girl, production photograph by Kristina Fosmire

Disillusioned by years of social rejection, a wannabe artist spurns the surface world to embark on her greatest project yet: a massive, sprawling, underground tunnel. As she digs deeper into her work, searching for self-actualization and clawing at any semblance of control, she finds herself buried in an all-too-familiar misery.

Tunnel Play is a fifty-minute solo play about desperation. Critics have described Tunnel Play as “gunky, gross, and heartbreakingly, comically honest” (Ava O’Dea, New Play Exchange). It was first produced by the Spade Collective to a sold-out Brick Aux in 2024, directed by L.D. Kelley, managed by Kristina Fosmire, and starring Charlotte Teresa Guerrero. The script is available through New Play Exchange. Contact me directly for licensing information.

Tunnel Play is my playwriting debut, and I’m very proud of it. It’s a great testament to my ability to work collaboratively with other artists. It also demonstrates my ability to work quickly — Charlotte and I wrote this play on a three week deadline while simultaneously very busy with other work. Most importantly, it’s shown me that my art can connect with a broad audience, and is potentially professionally viable.

Pope Gonzo

Pope Gonzo is an Instagram project that is equal parts Muppet fan page and commentary on the Catholic Church, and an exercise in both photo editing and illustration. It answers the age old question “What if Gonzo was elected Pope?.” It shows Gonzo fulfilling papal duties, meeting with world leaders, kissing babies, and even receiving a custom vestment on his trip to Japan. Pope Gonzo has inspired fan art, and has raised money for protest bail funds through merchandise sales. The project began in 2019 and is ongoing, but is currently on an indefinite hiatus.

Pope Gonzo is a great example of my skills in photo editing and digital art, as well as asset management (I maintain a collection of dozens of photos of Gonzo’s head at different angles). It also demonstrates my ability to create and adhere to a style guide. To remain consistent, I make sure that no other Muppets appear in “official” Pope Gonzo materials, that Gonzo is the same size as his human counterparts, and that assets are pulled from later Muppets materials, as the puppet’s design has changed over time. This project has also made me well-versed in working with print-on-demand merchandise companies.

Skate Comics

The Skate comics are a series I started in 2023 (https://www.instagram.com/clownpantscomics/?hl=en). They’re about a goofy beast named Skate, who loves to skate, and his weird stoner friends. The Skate comics are a sort of surrealist, zine-like project, featuring a range of media, from physical drawings, to digital collage, to animation. They’re silliness in its purest form. Themes of the Skate comics include wacky hijinks, taking it easy, having fun with friends, moving slowly, and not taking yourself too seriously. None of the animals in this series have ever experienced a deadline. The series is currently on hiatus.

Working on these comics has allowed me to loosen up in my art and to branch out into a range of styles. I’ve tried to make the characters have distinctive features, so they can shapeshift a bit without losing recognizability. Although I’ve been taking a break from the series, I have dozens of ideas for future Skate comics. It’s been wonderful to see how a small idea can give me so much inspiration.
